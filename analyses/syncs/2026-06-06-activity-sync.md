## The course — fixed 21 km loop, three terrain sections

This is a course Patrik runs regularly for 21 km, so it's a reusable benchmark — but it is **not flat-steady**, and averaging across it is misleading. Three sections (boundaries from the distance/altitude streams):

| Section | Terrain | Dist | Elev | Raw pace | GAP | Avg HR |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A** (0–7 km) | runnable road/path | 7.0 km | +76 m | 5:23 | 5:23 | 133.5* |
| **B** (7–15.5 km) | **mixed runnable/trail** | 8.5 km | **+164 m** | 5:40 | 5:26 | 140.4 |
| **C** (15.5–21.3 km) | runnable | 5.8 km | +82 m | 5:12 | **5:03** | 145.0 |

\*A includes the cold-start warmup (HR 80→130 over ~1.5 km); A's steady portion is GAP 5:22 / HR 135.8.

**Terrain confirmed:** Section B carries the bulk of the climb (164 of the 237 m) and most of any technical footing — its raw pace (5:40) is ~14 s/km slower than its GAP (5:26), so most of B's apparent slowdown is just the hills. B's HR is only mid-pack (140), not a spike. So the middle isn't a hard effort; it's terrain.

## The drift survives terrain correction (the interesting part)

The clean apples-to-apples is **Section A vs Section C — both runnable**, compared at matched grade-adjusted pace (same effort net of grade):

| Matched GAP band | A's HR | C's HR |
|---|---|---|
| 2.8–3.0 m/s | 135.6 | 144.0 |
| 3.0–3.2 m/s | 135.8 | 145.0 |
| 3.2–3.4 m/s | 136.5 | 145.4 |
| 3.4–3.6 m/s | 135.3 | 144.0 |

At **every** matched-effort band C runs ~9 bpm hotter than A → **~0.6 bpm/km of genuine cardiac drift**, close to the whole-run 0.85 figure. So terrain did *not* manufacture the drift — it's real.

**Reconciling with Patrik's "it was pace, not fatigue" read:** partly right. Section C was the **fastest section of the day** (GAP 5:03 vs A's 5:22, ~19 s/km quicker) — a deliberate negative-split fast finish, and a faster finish does lift HR. So the raw A→C jump (+11.5 bpm) is partly just C working harder. But once you strip that out by matching GAP, ~9 bpm of drift remains. **Both things are true at once:** a genuine fast-finish push *and* real B2B cardiac drift underneath it. (Note: a standard efficiency-factor decoupling reads a misleadingly benign +0.3% here precisely because C ran fast enough to hold its speed/HR ratio — the matched-pace lens is the correct one and it says drift.)

It stays a **non-issue** — legs felt fine, zero time near threshold, and 0.6 bpm/km on the second of two back-to-back longs is exactly what you'd expect. But it is real fatigue signal, not a terrain artifact and not purely pace.

## Megablast ultra-suitability — now in real doubt (the headline)

Earlier syncs treated the open Megablast question as narrowly "does the toe-box pinch like May 29." Patrik has now widened it into a genuine **is-this-an-ultra-shoe** doubt, with two distinct concerns for the 160 km goal race:

1. **Aggressive propulsion is form-dependent.** The Megablast is great *when he keeps his hips up / core engaged* — but the moment he relaxes, the assist drops off. He doesn't believe he can hold that posture for 160 km. So over ultra distance the shoe's aggression risks becoming a **liability** (working against a tired, collapsed late-race posture) rather than the help it is at 21 km on fresh-ish legs. This is the more important of the two — it's structural to the shoe, not a fit detail.
2. **Narrow toe-box, latent risk.** No sharp pain today (matches the "mild/different, not the May-29 pinch" read), but narrowness is exactly the kind of thing that surfaces **deep in an ultra** when the foot swells (hour 15+ — the very reason the Evo SL Silver was bought a size up). Absence of acute pain at 21 km does **not** clear it for 160 km.

**Implication for gear strategy:** the Megablast's role narrows toward **quality and medium-long sessions** — where form holds and the distance is short enough that posture-collapse and toe-box swell don't bite. For the **160 km Berlin Mauerweglauf**, the **size-up Evo SL Silver** (`g31397925`, roomy, less aggressive) stays the lead candidate; the Megablast is looking less like an ultra-race shoe. This is a forward gear question to settle at the next review (and a note added to the Megablast config entry, committed separately).
