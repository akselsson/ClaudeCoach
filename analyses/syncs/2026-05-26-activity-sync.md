## What this resolves and what it doesn't

**Resolved.** The May 21 sync's "is this a one-off or a pattern?" question on solo runs creeping to steady. Answer: pattern, and it correlates with the shoes (timing of "last 4–5 runs" matches). Not a fitness issue, not a heat issue — a pace-perception issue.

**Not resolved.** Berlin Mauerweglauf (Aug 15, 161 km road) shoe strategy. Patrik raised the concern directly. At 161 km, conversational pace is everything — a shoe that erodes easy-effort discipline is genuinely the wrong tool for the back half of an ultra. The "free pace" benefit is also the smallest where it matters least: ultra race pace is a slow shuffle (~6:30–7:30/km), and energy return / responsiveness at that pace is dominated by cushion and protection, not by ground-feel snap.

The shoe-rotation framing worth thinking about (raised, not decided):
- **Daily / recovery** — soft, durable, *not* these. Old pair, or a high-stack max-cushion trainer. The shoe Patrik runs in when he wants HR to stay <140 should *prevent* it from drifting to 5:00/km.
- **Workout / tempo** — these new shoes likely belong here. They earn their pace at threshold/interval effort, where 5:00/km is the target.
- **Race / long** — for Mauerweglauf, max-cushion road shoe (Hoka Bondi / Asics Nimbus / Brooks Glycerin class), broken in across the next 12 weeks. Long runs from June onward should rotate them in.

Patrik needs to weigh in on what shoe he actually bought, what he was thinking re ultra, and how many pairs he wants to manage. The plan can adapt once that's known.
