## Mon Jul 13 — Day 1, Hemavan → Tärnasjöstugorna (40.3 km)

- **Distance/time:** 40.35 km, 6:08 moving / 6:13 elapsed (only ~5 min auto-pause — walk breaks and hiking counted as *moving*), **1073 m vert**. **HR:** avg 135.6 / max 159. **Load:** TRIMP 572 / Edwards 597 / suffer 248, ~3639 kcal.
- **Characterization:** easy-to-steady long mountain endurance — the tool tagged `steady_aerobic` off the 36% steady share, but with avg HR 135 and half the day sub-140 it reads as an **easy/steady ultra-long day**: the stimulus is *duration*, not intensity. Zones: **easy (<140) 51.9%, steady (140–150) 36.4%, sub-threshold (150–160) 11.8%, threshold/vo2 0%** — ~88% at or below steady, peak only touched 159, never crossed 160.
- **Climbs hiked, as instructed** ("Gick uppförsbackar"), which is exactly why 1073 m of vert only nudged HR into the 150–160 band and never to threshold — the vert was absorbed by walking, keeping the whole day aerobic. ~44% of samples at walking pace.
- **Back-half read (the Achilles note):** no cardiac fade — HR actually *declined* in the second half (139.6 → 133.5, slope −0.24 bpm/km) while pace slowed. That HR-down + pace-down + more-walking pattern is him **backing off in the last ~10 km of birch forest**, consistent with the noted Achilles sensation ("Sega sista 10km i björkskog med känning i hälsena"). Reduced effort / protective management — **not overreaching.** "Annars fantastisk dag."
- **vs plan:** recut penciled ~36 km / ~7 h, Z1–Z2 (<150). Landed **longer at 40.3 km**, ~6 h moving, aerobically bang-on. ✅ Day 1 banked; the one flag is the Achilles, not the shins (below).

## Tue Jul 14 — Day 2, Tärnasjöstugorna → Ammarnäs (41.5 km) — the feature of the trip

- **Distance/time:** 41.47 km, 6:09 moving / 6:35 elapsed (~26 min stops, 93% moving), **1320 m vert — *more* climbing than Day 1.** **HR:** avg 124.4 / max 144. **Load:** TRIMP 446 / Edwards 407 / suffer 99, ~3418 kcal.
- **Characterization:** the tool tagged `recovery` — and that undersells the *effort* while nailing the *signal*. A 41.5 km / 1320 m / 6 h day executed **entirely at recovery HR**: **easy (<140) 97%, steady 3%, everything above 0%**, max only 144 (peak effort ~78% of max HR).
- **The headline — HR suppression on pre-fatigued legs:** avg HR **124.4 vs 135.6 on Day 1 — a full 11 bpm lower** — despite +1 km distance and **+250 m more vert**, with max capped 15 bpm lower (144 vs 159). On fresh legs, 1320 m of climbing would push HR into steady/tempo on the ascents; that it stayed pinned sub-140 all day is the **glycogen-depletion / neuromuscular-fatigue HR ceiling in action.** This is exactly the back-half-of-the-race state the recut wanted to rehearse — a single fresh long run can't reproduce it.
- **Aerobic durability:** zero cardiac drift across 6 h on tired legs — first-half avg 125.2, second-half 125.4 (~0 bpm/km). Pace quickened in the back half (front-loaded climb → later descent, terrain not fitness). A clean durability marker.
- **vs plan:** recut penciled ~41 km / ~10 h, Z1–Z2. **Distance bang-on (41.5)**; came in ~6.6 h elapsed vs the conservative 10 h estimate — GPS almost certainly stopped during hut/food breaks, and the moving terrain was faster than budgeted. ✅ Day 2 banked, and it's the single most valuable session of the build so far for what it rehearses.
