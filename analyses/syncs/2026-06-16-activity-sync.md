- **Effort came in one zone hot.** The characterizer tags it **steady_aerobic**, not easy: ~62% of time in the steady band (140–150), ~33% truly easy (<140), ~6% sub-threshold, **0% at threshold or above**. - **Read:** a clean, well-distributed steady-aerobic run, ~5–10 bpm above the easy ceiling — not the recovery-pace Z2 the plan called for, but with zero top-end contamination and no drift it carries no risk. **The more useful signal is what it says about recovery:** the plan offered a soft-start (8–10 km / jog) if the birthday/Midsummer-weekend debt and the high RHR-48 reading were still lingering. The weekend confound looks to be clearing.

### Gear: Megablast for the intervals — the predicted ad-hoc reach, and a broken-in data point

He ran the **ASICS Megablast**, not the planned Endorphin Speed. This is exactly the use the Jun 9 sync set up: the Megablast is **unpinned from any fixed slot** — Endorphin Speed is the default interval shoe, the Megablast is the **ad-hoc, when-he-feels-like-it** option. Reaching for it on a VO2 Tuesday is fine and costs nothing in training terms: on VO2 intervals the stimulus is set by physiological intensity (HR/VO2), not by the pace the shoe produces, so the Megablast's propulsion buys faster splits, not more training effect.

It also feeds the **open watch from Jun 9** — whether the Megablast's character (propulsion, toe-box) shifts once it's properly broken in, since the earlier read was on fresh foam.
