## What Patrik's A/B reveals

**Patrik's stated read: Evo SL may be easier to run slow in than the Megablast.** That lines up with what the numbers show — at 2 bpm lower HR than the May 26 Megablast run, the Evo SL ran 12 s/km slower, and HR drift was much smaller (0.68 vs the Megablast's drift-to-steady pattern). The Megablast's rocker bias *wants* a faster cadence; the Evo SL's flatter geometry and lower stack apparently doesn't push back as hard when you ask for a recovery pace.

This is one data point and one impression. It is not yet enough to rewrite the rotation. But it does sharpen the question the rotation was built to answer:

- The May 26 rotation framed the Megablast as the offender for easy-pace drift, and **Altra Torin as the antidote**. The Evo SL was scoped to Block 2 long-only.
- Today suggests Evo SL might split that easy-pace antidote role with the Torin. If it does, the practical case for the Evo SL strengthens (one less shoe in active rotation, more Block 2 mileage before race day, fewer "which pair today?" decisions).
- It does **not** weaken the case for keeping the Megablast on Tue quality and Fri Z2 — that's where the rocker earns its keep, and today's run wasn't a test of that.

What's needed before promoting Evo SL into easy-day duty:

1. **Jun 4 fit-check at 14 km steady (HR 140–150) holds its purpose** — *and* it's now framed as an A/B against the Megablast's natural zone. Recovery comfort doesn't generalize to sustained pace; the upsized fit specifically risks sloppiness on longer wears. No promotion before that test runs.
2. **At least one more recovery-pace data point in the Evo SL** to confirm today wasn't a one-off. The simplest path is to rotate Evo SL into the next easy day in lieu of Torin (e.g. Sun May 31 if Patrik runs, or next Thu mid-week).
3. **No fit complaint reported today** (no heel lift, midfoot slop, or toe-box notes — and suffer 49 / drift 0.68 are both clean). Soft positive on short-distance comfort. Still doesn't override the rotation's specific warning that upsized shoes need sustained-pace load to reveal sloppiness.

The Megablast → Fri 28 km Z2 plan tomorrow is unchanged. That's the next test of the rotation as written, and the comparison point for whether the rocker is helping or pushing at sustained Z2.
