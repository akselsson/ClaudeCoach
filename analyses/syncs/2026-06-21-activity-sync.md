## Sun Jun 21 — anchor long run (32.0 km) — the hot one

- **Distance/time:** 32.0 km, 3:13:33 moving (~6:03/km avg) — longest run by time in 10 weeks. **HR:** avg 133 / max 163. **Suffer:** 114 (TRIMP 294 / Edwards 305).
- **Gear:** Adidas Evo SL Silver — third road long in the shoe, and the longest sustained load in it yet.
- **Structure:** "Första 20,5 med Emma sen själv" — first 20.5 km easy with Emma, then solo. Clean negative split that maps exactly onto the partner split.
  - First half: HR **125** @ **6:20/km** → second half: HR **142** @ **5:30/km** (~50 s/km faster solo).
- **Heat read (the key one):** the raw +17.6 bpm first-vs-second-half gap looks alarming but is **paced, not heat-driven**. The back half was run ~50 s/km faster, so most of the HR rise is the pace increase. Normalized HR slope **1.18 bpm/km — below the 1.5 bpm/km "meaningful drift" line**, and that's generous given pace rose at the same time. Decoupling well-controlled. The heat's cost shows only as the run drifting above Z2 (~31% of time crept into 140–160), never reaching threshold (0.7% threshold, 0% VO2).
- **Zones:** Z2/easy **68.8%**, sub-threshold 15.9%, steady 14.6%, threshold 0.7%, VO2 0%. Effort tag: steady_aerobic / focus long_endurance — the "steady" comes entirely from the solo finish, the bulk was genuinely easy.
- **Load vs recent longs:** longest by time, yet suffer (114) is *lower* than the comparable Jun 5 31.6 km (213) and May 29 29.3 km (168). Avg HR 133 is the lowest of any 20 km+ effort bar the deliberately-very-easy ones. Low suffer-per-km despite heat and length — a well-controlled aerobic long that lifted cleanly in the back half. (The Apr 18 104 km Tuscany ultra is excluded from this comparison as a non-comparable outlier.)
- **vs plan:** the plan called for ~26–32 km Z2 (HR 140–150) on the Evo SL Silver as "the week's anchor and the last structured long before Italy." Landed at the **top of the range, 32 km**, executed at controlled effort in the heat. ✅ Anchor banked.

## Open watch — Evo SL Silver shins

The Jun 14 review flagged this run specifically: "watch shins/medial-tibia specifically on this one — it's the longest sustained load in the shoe yet." **Subjective read (Patrik, sync-time): felt the shin but no pain — sensation only, manageable for now.** That's a yellow flag, not red: the Evo SL *is* producing a shin signal at 32 km, consistent with the Adidas Boston analogy, but it stayed below pain. Watch stays open and is now upgraded from "theoretical" to "confirmed mild signal" — escalation to actual pain on an Evo SL long would reopen the race-shoe question.

This prompted a **gear-rotation rethink** (Patrik's instinct): reserve the Evo SL Silver for race day + the race-specific long efforts, and move the bulk of easy/daily volume into the more cushioned daily trainers (Torin) to keep the Evo SL's wear low and give the shins more support on routine miles. Refined recommendation below — **not yet ratified into the plan** (next formal review is on return from Italy).

### Proposed rotation (pending ratification)
- **Evo SL Silver → race day + the long runs / big-specificity weekends only.** Rationale runs both ways: (a) for a ~160 km A-race (Berlin Mauerweglauf, Aug 15) you *cannot* race in a shoe unproven at duration, so it must keep getting the long-run miles; (b) but it does **not** need easy daily mileage — those miles only accumulate wear (the exact thing the Boston analogy says seeds shin trouble) without giving race-relevant data. So "race day only" is too far; "longs + key sessions only" is the sweet spot.
- **Torin (or other cushioned daily trainer) → all easy/recovery/by-feel volume.** More support under the shins on the bulk of mileage, and keeps the Evo SL fresher and lower-wear into August.
- **Near-term (Italy, Jun 22+):** flip the Italy gear emphasis — **Torin as the default for easy/by-feel runs, Evo SL only for the one longer free-form effort** (the Cinque Terre long), if it happens. This supersedes the Jun 14 review's "Evo SL Silver primary + Torin for easy days" for the deload window.
- **Trigger to revisit the race shoe entirely:** shin sensation crossing into pain on an Evo SL long. There's still runway to Aug 15 to pivot (Tecton X3 etc.) if it does.
