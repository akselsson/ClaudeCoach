## The "hot at the end" — real but normal

Patrik felt hot at the end, and the data confirms a genuine back-half lift — but it's textbook late-long-run aerobic creep, not overreaching:

- **First half avg HR 137.3 → second half 145.9 (+8.6 bpm)**, while pace actually **quickened** 331.7 → 325.2 s/km (~5:32 → 5:25/km). Faster *and* warmer in the back half.
- HR sat ~135–140 through km 12, ticked up from km 14, and **parked at 147–150 for the entire final ~8 km** (km 22–29). That settled 147–150 plateau is the warmth he felt.
- **Whole-run drift slope only 0.54 bpm/km** — comfortably under the 1.5 bpm/km cardiac-drift concern line. This is controlled aerobic drift plus a lightly lifted pace, not a fade and not a hard finish.
- The final km (29) was the **fastest of the whole run at 4:57/km** and still only 148 bpm — legs had more, HR stayed in check. That's a strong sign, not a worrying one.

The 16.4% in sub-threshold (150–160) is the only time above the Z2 ceiling, and it's all in that final third. For a 29 km run on 299 m of climb, drifting from low-Z2 to 147–150 by the back 8 km is expected and benign. No threshold stimulus (0.3% above 160, incidental).

## Race-shoe signal — pinky toes at 29 km (the important one)

The Megablast passed the *training-zone* test (above). But the gear feel exposed the question the rotation flagged but couldn't yet answer: **is the Megablast a viable ultra race shoe?** Today says: not in its current fit.

- The Megablast is worn in **normal fit**. The Evo SL was bought **one size up specifically for foot swell at hour 15+** — toe-box pinch, blisters, and black toenails at km 100+ are classic ultra DNF causes (`schedule/2026-05-26-shoe-rotation.md`, "Why Evo SL is the leading ultra candidate"). The size-up *is* the feature.
- **Pinky toes complaining at 29 km is early and concrete.** Berlin Mauerweglauf is ~161 km. If toe-box pressure is noticeable at km 29 on fresh-ish feet, it will be worse with the foot swell that builds over 15+ hours. This is exactly the failure mode the Evo SL size-up was bought to prevent and the normal-fit Megablast structurally can't address.
- **What this does NOT do:** override the deferred race-shoe verdict (still after the 60–70 km specificity runs, ~Jul 20). One 29 km run is one data point.
- **What this DOES do:** put a real, datable strike against the Megablast as the *race* shoe, and strengthen the Evo SL's case on the single dimension (toe-box / swell tolerance) that the rotation already called the dominant factor. It also sharpens the Jun 4 A/B fit-check — that run should now explicitly compare toe-box comfort, not just HR/pace.

The Megablast keeps its **training** role (Tue quality, Fri Z2 long) — toe pressure at training distances is tolerable and the rocker earns its keep in Z2. This is purely about the 100-mile race-day shoe.
