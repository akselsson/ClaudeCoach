# Race review — Kungsholmen Runt, Sat May 9

Reviewing against `2026-05-07-weekly-review.md` (race-week mid-check) and the original race-day plan in `2026-05-03-block-1-weeks-may4-jun7.md`.

## What happened

- **Distance / time:** 21.20 km in 1:29:03, 4:12/km average.
- **HR:** avg **167**, max **182** (99% of HR max 184).
- **Load:** TRIMP **271**, Edwards **376**, Strava suffer **274**. Largest single-session load in the 14-day window by a wide margin (~5× the May 5 tempo, ~2.7× the Apr 26 21k easy long run).
- **Zones:** 80.7% of the run at/above threshold; **44.7%** in vo2 (HR >170, ~40 min). Effectively a half-marathon time trial.
- **Lap shape:** 22 auto-1 km laps, lap-pace CV 0.028. Steady continuous, no deliberate intervals, no surges.
- **Split:** First half avg HR **160.6**, second half **173.5** → **+12.9 bpm**. Pace first half 4:16/km, second half **4:09/km**. Negative split *with* a large HR rise — pushed harder progressively, finished at vo2 effort.

## How that maps to the race plan

The mid-week race plan (May 7) called for: HR ≤160 km 0–7, 160–170 km 7–17, cap off km 17–21, gels km 8 + 15, no sprint finish.

| Plan element | Actual | Read |
|---|---|---|
| HR ≤160 km 0–7 | First-half avg 160.6 | Held the cap (avg right at it) — opening was disciplined. |
| HR 160–170 km 7–17 | Mid-race climbed into the 170s | Drifted above the ceiling earlier than planned. |
| Cap off km 17–21 | Max 182 in the back end | Used the cap-off zone as designed. |
| Negative or even split | Negative (4:16 → 4:09) | ✓ Hit cleanly. |
| No sprint finish | Lap CV 0.028, no end-spike | ✓ Steady push, not a kick. |
| No HR runaway by km 12 | Pace got *faster* while HR climbed | ✓ Fitness-limited fade pattern, not the bad runaway shape. |

**Read on the race:** the shape is what the plan asked for — even-or-negative split, no surge finish, no sharp pace collapse — and the bail criteria (sharp pain, runaway HR-to-pace) were not tripped. The HR ran above the 160–170 ceiling earlier than the plan modeled, but the back third was explicitly cap-off, so the highest readings were within plan envelope. Average HR 167 against a 165 race-effort cap is "right at the line." Max 182 is high but lands inside the final cap-off km.

The thing the data tells me clearly: **this was a real race, not a controlled effort.** 45% in vo2 zone for 90 min puts this at the top of any session this block. The recovery cost is correspondingly larger than a tightly-capped HR-165 race would have been.

## What's good about the performance

- **Negative split with HR drift but no pace fade.** That's the textbook well-paced half — the body still had output to give in the back half, and you used it. Compare to the failure mode (pace falling off while HR climbs) which would have signalled under-recovery.
- **No surge finish.** Hard rule held. This is what protects calves/Achilles after a 100k 24 days back.
- **Pace 4:12/km off a recovery block.** No quality work in 8 of the last 10 days specifically aimed at this race; the leg-speed touches (Apr 28 4×1km, May 2 2×3km, May 5 4×4min) were enough.
- **Bail criteria not tripped.** Per the strava data alone (no pause, steady cadence, finished the route), the race went without a sharp-pain abort. (User to confirm subjective state — see "What I'm watching for" below.)

## What's the cost

- **TRIMP 271 is a real bill.** Week 2 was already planned as recovery; that plan is now even more correct.
- **80% above threshold for 90 min** loads the same systems as a tempo + interval session combined. The aerobic stress is 21k worth, the muscular stress is closer to a long tempo.
- **High back-half drift (+13 bpm).** Cardiovascular reserve was clearly tapped — not a "sandbagged easy effort." Reads as a genuine race performance, which means the recovery curve looks more like post-race than post-controlled-effort.

## Discrepancy flag for the season record

The May 7 review's tightened opening cap (≤160 km 0–7) was hit exactly; the 160–170 mid-race ceiling was crossed earlier than planned. Not a "deviation" in any meaningful sense — race-day HR rarely lands tidy inside three discrete bands — but worth noting that **the day-of HR average sat above the 165 race-effort cap that Block 1 set as a red line for non-race days.** That cap exists for a reason: any day in training above it = abort. The race was the carve-out it was written for, and the carve-out got used.
