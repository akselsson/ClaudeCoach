# Schedule note — Italy window (Jun 22 – Jul 6), the unstructured deload

## How to run it

**Principle: unstructured maintenance.** No prescribed sessions, no weekly target, no quality obligation. Keep the aerobic system ticking and let two-plus weeks of disrupted social/family travel do their job as a deload. The trip and family come first at every single point — every run below is droppable.

- **Jun 22 (travel day):** optional easy shakeout in Stockholm before the evening flight, or just travel. Off is fine.
- **Lake Como (Jun 22–26):** hilly terrain — easy runs by feel on the non-hike mornings. The **Jun 24 family hike is the day's time-on-feet and vert** (genuinely useful for Berlin's modest-but-cumulative profile) — no separate run that day. Don't chase pace on the climbs; HR runs high on mountain gradients, so go by effort, not the watch.
- **Cinque Terre / Lavagna (Jun 26 – Jul 3):** the most open week. If legs feel good and a morning window opens, this is the natural slot for **one longer run (a relaxed ~2–2.5 h on the coastal trails)** — but as free-form time on feet, not a structured long. Otherwise easy runs by feel. Steep coastal trails = free hill strength; walk the steep bits like you will in Berlin.
- **Viareggio (Jul 3–5, family only):** flat coast — short easy flat runs along the seafront if a window appears, else off. Family days; expect mostly off.
- **Milan (Jul 5–6):** Jul 6 morning before the 21:55 flight is an easy-run option if you want one.

## Shoes

Bring **one easy-day pair (Torin)** and, if packing room allows, the **Megablast** in case a longer Cinque Terre effort happens. No quality sessions planned, so a single versatile pair is genuinely enough — pack light.

## After the return

Structured training resumes the week of **Jul 6**. The next weekly review (written on return, once we see how the body responds to two weeks of unstructured travel + hill hiking) picks up the **specificity phase**: the first big back-to-back weekend (≈50 km + 35 km), night-running practice, and race-food rehearsal, all inside the work-leave window through Aug 18. Berlin Mauerweglauf (161 km) is **Aug 15**.

## Watch list (carried)

Calves/Achilles, pinky-toe/toe-box, RHR, long-run drift — unchanged from the May-30 review. Italy *sheds* structured load, so nothing new added; the one thing to watch is steep-downhill quad load on the Como/Cinque Terre trails (eccentric, easy to overdo on holiday legs) — ease into the descents.
