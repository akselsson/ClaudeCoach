# Weekly review — Italy deload week 1 (Jun 22–30)

Reviewing against the **Italy unstructured deload** (`schedule/2026-06-05-italy-deload-window.md`, two weeks confirmed Jun 22 → home Jul 6 by `schedule/2026-06-21-italy-gear-update.md`), with `syncs/2026-06-21-activity-sync.md` (the 32 km anchor long + first confirmed mild shin signal) as the last thing on record before departure. **No prescribed sessions in this window** — so this is not a plan-vs-actual reconciliation; it's a read on how the body took week 1 of the deload and how we re-enter specificity on return.

Per the standing instruction (now in memory): all activity types were pulled, not just runs. The two hikes are a material part of the load here and would have been invisible to a Run-only review.

## How the last 8 days went

**Week 1 of the Italy deload did its job — and then some on the hills.** Eight days, six easy runs + two big-vert hikes, every session by feel:

| Date | Type | Dist | Vert | HR avg/max | Note |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Jun 22 | Run | 10.3 km | 196 m | 136/154 | Stockholm a.m. before the evening flight |
| Jun 23 | Run | 3.9 km | 19 m | 111/127 | "Handla frukost" — a jog-errand, trivial |
| Jun 24 | **Hike** | 6.1 km | **486 m** | 93/136 | Family: Rifugio Menaggio + half Monte Grona |
| Jun 25 | Run | 7.0 km | 51 m | 135/152 | Easy |
| Jun 27 | Run | 8.9 km | 320 m | 131/163 | "Bread run with detour" |
| Jun 28 | Run | 5.8 km | 328 m | 144/168 | 🥵 — short, hot, steep |
| Jun 29 | **Hike** | 8.1 km | **484 m** | 101/147 | Cinque Terre: Monterosso→Corniglia |
| Jun 30 | Run | 9.2 km | 395 m | 127/155 | "På upptäcksfärd" — exploring |

**The load shape is the headline, and it's exactly what Patrik flagged.** Running volume dropped hard — **36 km (Jun 22 week) vs 75 km the week before** — a genuine *cardiovascular* deload (suffer scores 5–55, avg HR mostly 111–144, nothing near threshold). But **elevation went up**: ~914 m of climb in the runs plus ~970 m across the two hikes ≈ **~1,880 m of climb/descent in 8 days**. So this was a cardiovascular deload but **not a musculoskeletal one** — the legs absorbed a lot of eccentric (descent) load. That's good Berlin-specific prep (cumulative time-on-feet + descents), and it reframes "rested": the legs are aerobically fresh but have been working downhill all week.

No session here needed per-session characterization — all runs read as plainly easy/deload aerobic, the hikes as low-HR time-on-feet. The only run tripping a flag was **Jun 28** (🥵, HR 144/168, suffer 55, max−avg gap 24), and at 5.8 km with 328 m of climb in the heat that's unambiguously a short hot hill grind, not disguised quality — no drilldown warranted. Deciding *not* to drill in, on the basis of activity type, is itself the call the standing instruction asks for.

**Subjective state (Patrik, Jun 30):**
1. **Legs feel good** — taking the ~1,880 m of vert well, no lingering quad soreness despite all the descending.
2. **Shins have *liked* the transfer to hilly running** — i.e. the Evo SL parked + the cushioned Lone Peak on soft hilly terrain has been actively kind to the shins. This is the strongest possible signal that the rest is working (see gear ratification below).
3. **Sleep / energy about the same** as a normal week — holiday hasn't degraded recovery.
4. **Too hot for long runs** — heat is the binding constraint, which is why the optional Cinque Terre long never happened.
5. **Megablast** broken-in read still inconclusive — "hard to say with the elevation profile and primarily short runs." Watch stays open.

## Discrepancies and decisions

No plan to deviate from (unstructured deload), so these are decisions, not gaps:

- **The optional Cinque Terre ~2–2.5 h free-form long did not happen — and that's correct.** Reason: too hot. **Decision:** accept fully. The two big hikes (486 m + 484 m of vert) were the week's long efforts; forcing a hot long would have cost more than it bought. The first real long is the re-entry long back in (cooler) Stockholm.
- **Gear plan executed cleanly.** New **Altra Lone Peak 9+** (`g31943887`, now ~30 km, all Italy) took the family hikes + hilly trail runs; **Megablast** (`g31149428`) took the flatter road runs (Jun 25, Jun 30); **Evo SL Silver stayed home** as planned, giving the shin-watch shoe a full 8-day rest. **Decision:** ratify the rotation (below). Lone Peak 9+ registered in `config/training.json` (separate commit).
- **Jun 27 "bread run" logged against the "Unbranded/Okategoriserat" shoe** (`g1902237`). Almost certainly just an unset shoe field, not a real mystery pair. **Decision:** noted, no action — low-stakes.
- **RHR clean re-read (carried open since the Jun 14 review's confounded 48).** Still not captured cleanly. **Decision:** low priority on holiday; re-check on return when back on routine.

## Gear rotation — RATIFIED

The Jun 21 sync proposed reserving the Evo SL Silver for race day + long efforts and moving easy/daily volume to cushioned trainers, explicitly "to be ratified or revised at the Italy-return review." **This review ratifies it.** The deciding evidence is read #2: with the Evo SL parked and the bulk of miles on cushioned shoes over hilly terrain, **the shins didn't just stay quiet — they improved.** That's the rotation's thesis confirmed in the most direct way available.

**Standing rotation from here:**
- **Evo SL Silver → race day + the long runs / big-specificity weekends only.** It still *must* keep getting long-duration miles (you cannot race 160 km in a shoe unproven at duration), so it goes back on under the re-entry long — but it no longer eats easy daily mileage, which only accumulates the wear the Adidas-Boston analogy says seeds shin trouble.
- **Cushioned daily trainers → all easy/recovery/by-feel volume.** Lone Peak 9+ for trail, Torin / Megablast for road.
- **Trigger to reopen the race-shoe question:** shin sensation crossing from *felt* into *pain* on an Evo SL long. Runway to Aug 15 still allows a pivot (Tecton X3) if it does.
