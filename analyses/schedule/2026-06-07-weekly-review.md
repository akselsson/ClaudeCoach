## How the last 8 days went

**Block 1 closed exactly where the rebuild aimed.** The two open questions from the May-30 review — would the first real quality in 3 weeks land cleanly, and would the body absorb the biggest long-run week of the build — both answered yes, and the RHR confirms it independently.

- **The build's first genuine quality happened (Tue Jun 2).** Prescribed a controlled threshold-leaning 4×4 with a hard back-off above HR 165; Patrik ran a real 4×4 that built progressively to HR 166 / max 175 (~41% of moving time at threshold-or-above) — a true VO2 session, not the threshold-lean the season plan intends. But this was a *deliberate* call on legs that felt ready, not the "group ran away with the paces" failure mode the prior reviews kept flagging. It answers the rebuild-readiness question with a clear yes.
- **The biggest long-run week of the build landed and was absorbed.** Fri Jun 5's 31.6 km Z2 was textbook — 66% in the 140–150 band, zero time above threshold, cardiac drift 0.36 bpm/km (under the May-29 baseline of 0.54, far under the 1.5 governor), no fade. The Week-5 governor ("hold at 30 km if Tuesday ran hot") was genuinely live — Tuesday *did* run hot — but never needed to fire. Sat Jun 6's 21.3 km back half (solo, terrain-corrected drift ~0.6 bpm/km on day-2 legs) closed a ~53 km B2B with legs reported fine.
- **RHR 44 — the cleanest signal of all.** Logged the night of Jun 6–7, *below* the May-7 reading of 46, after a quality + 53 km B2B week. That's not just "no fatigue accumulation" — it's a body that handled the hardest week of the build and came out fresher than its prior baseline. Strong green light into the disrupted travel weeks.

**Volume:** the week of Jun 1 closed at **67.7 km** against the ~78–80 km target. The ~12 km gap is almost exactly the missed Thursday 14 km (below) — not a shortfall in the sessions that mattered. Week of May 25 was 72.4 km. Both weeks tracked the plan within tolerance.

**Subjective state (Jun 7 check-in):** RHR 44, energy/motivation fine for the packed Lund + Midsummer stretch ahead. Pinky-toe and calves clear. **One new niggle: soreness under the midfoot** — something that has come and gone over the years; Patrik's read is it may need supportive insoles for a couple of runs. New watch item, built into the plan below.

The three Lund easy mornings (Jun 11–13), the Sun Jun 21 long, and the Italy longer efforts all move to the Evo SL Silver. **The Megablast is demoted to Tuesday quality only** (where form holds and the distance is short). Patrik confirmed he'll take the Evo SL to Lund.
- **New: midfoot soreness.** Recurring over the years, may need supportive insoles for a couple of runs. **Decision:** carry an insole option through the next 14 days; the size-up Evo SL has room for one. Watch that it doesn't escalate, especially as a new shoe (Evo SL) is introduced for the bulk of the Lund volume.
