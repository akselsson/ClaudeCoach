## How the last 14 days went

**The rebuild is done.** The two questions hanging over the post-100k + race recovery were "has aerobic fitness come back?" and "can the long run climb again?" — both answered yes, cleanly. Fri May 22's 24 km Z2 negative-split by grade-adjusted pace with zero cardiac drift; Fri May 29's 29 km Z2 held drift 0.54 bpm/km and produced its *fastest* km (4:57) as the final one at only 148 bpm. That's a body with more in the tank at the end of a long run, not one fading. The recovery checkpoint (Sun May 17, avg HR 118 with HR drifting *down*) had already flagged the 100k → race → interval-Tuesdays loading sequence as fully cleared, and nothing since has contradicted it.

**Volume tracked the plan.** Week 3 (May 18–24) landed ~64 km running across 4 runs; Week 4 (May 25–31) is at ~72 km through Saturday against a ~75 km target — modestly under, within tolerance, and not worth chasing with a Sunday make-up run. The first back-to-back weekend since Tuscany (Fri 29 km + Sat 21 km daughter Z1, ~50 km over two days with Saturday off between the prior Friday and this one) executed as a soft B2B exactly as designed.

**The build keeps running "easy" prescriptions a band hot — but it's now understood.** The May 19 (recovery → VO2, max 177) and May 21 (easy → steady) deviations, plus the May 26 substitute run, all landed in steady (140–150) when the plan said easy/recovery. The May 26 sync pinned the cause: the new Megablast delivers ~20 s/km of free pace at equal HR, and Patrik spends the gift on more pace rather than banking it as recovery. The shoe rotation (`2026-05-26`) was the structural fix; the May 28 Evo SL A/B (held 12 s/km slower at 2 bpm lower HR, drift 0.68) suggests it's working. This is a pace-perception story, not a fitness or fatigue one.

**The one real gap: still no genuine quality session at prescribed paces.** The May 15 plan had Tue May 26 as the *first proper quality of the build*. It didn't happen — group skipped for a scheduling conflict, and the solo substitute was steady, not quality. So 7 weeks past the 100k and 3 weeks past the race, the build has rebuilt its entire aerobic base but has not yet asked the body to hold a real threshold/interval effort on purpose (the May 19 VO2 was unplanned, at rebuild stage). That's the headline for the next two weeks.

**Subjective state (from today's check-in):** energy normal, no red flags, sleep and motivation fine. Calves and Achilles — the standing watch list through this whole loading sequence — are clear, including in the live 12–24 h window after Friday's first sustained Megablast long. The only niggle is **pinky-toe pressure that surfaced at 29 km in the Megablast and has lingered.** RHR last logged 46 (May 7); no fresher number this round — worth a reading early next week.
