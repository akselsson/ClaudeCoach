# Schedule note — Weeks 6 & 7 reshaped for back-to-back travel + Midsummer

Annotates and extends `schedule/2026-05-30-weekly-review.md` (which prescribed through Jun 13). Targeted adjustment to two weeks, not a new review — the season-plan shape and the post-Italy specificity intent are unchanged. Decided with Patrik on Jun 5 ahead of the trips.

## The stretch, framed

Three consecutive weeks are all disrupted, and that's fine — it's **one continuous taper-of-structure into the Italy deload**, which is exactly what the season plan wants here:

- **Week 6 (Jun 8–14)** — Stockholm→Lund travel + packed Thu–Sun social calendar.
- **Week 7 (Jun 15–21)** — Midsummer (Fri Jun 19) at the summer house + mother's birthday (Sat Jun 20) back in Stockholm.
- **Italy (~late June/early July, dates TBD)** — the planned unstructured maintenance/deload block.

The job for Weeks 6–7 is *not* to defend the season plan's long-run progression — that progression resumes after Italy, where the specificity big weekends (50+35, 60+30, 70+40) live in the work-leave window (Jun 18 – Aug 18). The job here is: keep aerobic volume ticking, protect the one Tuesday quality each week, and don't force a monster long run into a packed weekend. **Jun 5's 31.6 km clean Z2 is the peak single long for this whole pre-Italy stretch, and that's enough** — the 34 km was always a conditional +2, not load-bearing.

## Week 6 — Jun 8 to Jun 14 (Lund travel)

### The trip
- **Wed Jun 10** — travel Stockholm → Lund. Day fully off training.
- **Thu Jun 11** — studentmottagning.
- **Fri Jun 12** — at sister's, family time.
- **Sat Jun 13** — at an old friend's from after lunch.
- **Sun Jun 14** — travel home.

Social commitments are mostly midday/afternoon/evening, so morning runs before them work — but the week is packed and tired-after-a-long-run is the explicit thing to protect against.

### Revised Week 6

| Day | Session |
|---|---|
| Mon Jun 8 | Off. Easy spin OK if legs are restless. |
| **Tue Jun 9** (Stockholm) | **Group quality — the week's one real quality.** Megablast, threshold-leaning, 8–10 km. HR>165 abort rule. Heavy strength after. (If Kundo runs 4×4 min two weeks running, take it at threshold pace, not interval.) |
| **Wed Jun 10** | **Off — travel Stockholm→Lund.** Maps onto the already-planned rest/buffer day. |
| **Thu Jun 11** (Lund) | **~18–20 km easy, Z2 (HR 140–150), morning.** Before studentmottagning. |
| **Fri Jun 12** (Lund) | **~18–20 km easy, Z2, morning.** Before the family day. |
| **Sat Jun 13** (Lund) | **~18–20 km easy, Z2, morning.** Before the after-lunch friend visit. |
| **Sun Jun 14** | **Off — travel home.** |

**Shape is flexible by design (Patrik's call — read it on the ground):**
- Default is three even ~18–20 km easy mornings. If a good window opens on fresh legs, one may stretch to **~24–26 km** for a little long-run character — no obligation, never at the cost of the day's plans.
- **Any of the three is droppable or trimmable to 10–12 km** if a day gets eaten or legs read cooked from the day before. The trip comes first.
- Three consecutive easy mediums on cumulative-fatigue legs is a tidy bit of ultra-relevant time-on-feet on its own — no need to force the long run back in to "rescue" the week.

**Drop the 34 km (Fri Jun 12, per May-30 plan).** Near-zero cost: peak long already banked (Jun 5), daughter isn't in Lund so the weekend B2B partner is gone anyway, the decisive longs are post-Italy, and Italy is the next deload.

**Week target:** ~67 km (≈10 quality + 3×~19). Down from ~80 — correct for a disrupted social week, not a shortfall.

Not previously prescribed (May-30 review stopped at Jun 13). Work leave begins **Jun 18** — but Patrik works a full day from home that Thursday, so the Mon–Wed morning embargo still effectively governs Jun 15–17, and Thu Jun 18 is not a daytime run window.
