# Schedule update — first peak weekend (60+30) moves to Wed–Thu Jul 22–23

Supersedes the forward portion (Jul 20 onward) of `schedule/2026-07-12-kungsleden-b2b-recut.md`, which penciled the first peak weekend as a provisional **60+30 on Fri–Sat Jul 25–26**. Not a retrospective review — the recovery week (Jul 16–22) is executing as drafted: the two runs in `syncs/2026-07-18-activity-sync.md` (Jul 17 recovery jog, Jul 18 steady) show the legs jogging easy and tolerating a light steady run without shin or Achilles complaint. This locks a decision made in conversation on Jul 18: **run the 60+30 on Wed–Thu (Jul 22–23) instead of the weekend.**

## The decision

**The provisional 60+30 stays the same session — same distances, same shoes, same intent — it just moves three days earlier onto Wed–Thu.** This is the vacation-window B2B flex from `preferences/2026-05-03-training-preferences.md` (Jun 18 – Aug 18): with Kundo on summer break and the Mon/Wed morning embargo lifted, a back-to-back can land on any two consecutive days that fit the week. There is no Tuesday group-quality anchor to protect, so the "leave Tuesday clear on each side" guardrail doesn't bind — the same waiver the Kungsleden recut already invoked.

Why it's a good trade, not just an accommodation:

- **It evens out the spacing across the three big blocks.** Kungsleden was Jul 13–14. The provisional weekend placement gave **11 days** of recovery before the 60+30, then only **7 days** before the season's biggest weekend (70+40, Aug 1–2). Moving to Wed–Thu makes it **8 days then 10** — the *biggest* weekend now gets the longer runway, which is where the recovery margin matters most.
- **It gives each big block its own clean recovery tail.** recovery(Jul 16–21) → 60+30(Jul 22–23) → recovery/build(Jul 24–31) → 70+40(Aug 1–2). No block is jammed against the next.
- **The Wed–Thu shape mirrors the Kungsleden template** that just worked: big leg first, continuation on pre-fatigued legs. Same logic, road version.

## The cost, and how it's managed

Moving earlier cuts the Kungsleden absorption window from **11 → 8 days**. The Kungsleden B2B was the biggest two-day block of the build (~82 km / ~2393 m vert), and Day 2 ran at recovery HR (fatigue suppression = real depletion), with a transient Achilles note (resolved). And the 60 km leg is the **deferred Evo SL 50 km+ road shin-test** — a clean read wants *fresh* legs, and a genuine 60 km of road pounding on under-recovered legs raises injury risk.

**So the long leg is gated on the Mon–Tue leg-check, not committed blind:**

- **Legs fully bounced by Tue Jul 21** → run the full **60 km, Evo SL, road** — the shin-test happens as planned.
- **Legs coming back but not 100%** → **trim the long leg to ~45–50 km**, still Evo SL road (a partial but real shin read), keep the 30 km continuation.
- **Tuesday still reads heavy** (residual DOMS, elevated drift on the easy runs, any Achilles whisper) → **hold the 60+30 to the original Jul 25–26** rather than force a compromised effort that also muddies the shin read. Berlin > this weekend, always.

The Jul 18 steady run tolerated well (only a mild 1.45 bpm/km drift, cleanly explained by afternoon heat), and Kungsleden was run entirely aerobically — paced, not redlined — so 8 days is *defensible* for an experienced ultra runner on leave with no work stress. But it's earned at the Tue check-in, not assumed here.

- **The Mon–Tue leg-check reads.** These decide the long-leg distance (full 60 / trimmed 50 / hold to weekend). A steady easy run drifting at constant pace, or any DOMS/Achilles whisper, argues for trimming or holding.
- **The Evo SL 50 km+ road shin-test.** Still the open half of the shin double-read (Jul 8 34 km ✅ green). Wed Jul 22 is where it finally happens **if the legs earn the full 60** — a trimmed 45–50 km still gives a real, if shorter, road read. Any shin sensation → note it and it directly informs how hard to push the Aug 1 long.
- **Recovery into the 70+40 (Aug 1–2).** The whole point of moving earlier is to protect this. If the 60+30 lands Wed–Thu, the 8-day gap to the biggest weekend should be comfortable — confirm at the next review.
- **Fueling-under-fatigue on Thu.** The 30 km continuation on depleted legs is another Berlin rehearsal — track carb/h and gut tolerance, same as Kungsleden Day 2.

## What changed from the recut, and why

- **First peak weekend: Fri–Sat Jul 25–26 → Wed–Thu Jul 22–23.** Same 60+30 session; three days earlier. Rationale is block spacing (11-then-7 → 8-then-10), which gives the season's biggest weekend (70+40) the longer recovery runway. Enabled by the vacation-window B2B flex (Kundo on break, no Tuesday anchor).
- **The long-leg distance is now explicitly gated on the Mon–Tue leg-check** rather than assumed at 60 — the tighter 8-day post-Kungsleden window earns the full distance at the Tue read, or trims/holds.
- **Season macro unchanged.** 70+40 stays Aug 1–2, taper Aug 3–14, Berlin Mauerweglauf (161 km) Aug 15. Moving the 60+30 earlier does not touch the peak-weekend sequence or the taper — it only rebalances the recovery between them.
