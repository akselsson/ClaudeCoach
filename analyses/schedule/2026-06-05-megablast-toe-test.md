# Schedule note — Megablast toe-box test on the weekend daughter long run

Annotates `schedule/2026-05-30-weekly-review.md` (Week 5, Sat-or-Sun daughter long run). Not a re-prescription — the session is unchanged (~21 km, Z1, HR <140, her pick of day). Only the **shoe** changes, with an explicit test purpose.

## The swap

- **Prescribed:** ~21 km Z1 daughter long run, Torin.
- **Run instead in:** ASICS Megablast — deliberate pinky-toe / toe-box test.
- **Context:** Jun 5's 31.6 km long was run in the Torin *because* of the lingering pinky-toe fear (see `syncs/2026-06-05`), so the Megablast toe-box question from May 29 is still open — untested, not cleared.

## Why tomorrow is a good test (better than the distance looks)

- **Back half of a B2B → pre-loaded feet.** Feet start tomorrow already loaded from today's 31.6 km. Foot swelling accumulates across the weekend, so 21 km on day-2 feet plausibly reaches the swell state that ~29 km fresh produced on May 29 — a more honest proxy for the failure condition than a standalone fresh 21 km.
- **Z1 + social → lowest stakes.** Slow, easy, with my daughter — fully abortable (walk it out / cut short) at zero training cost. Far better to provoke the pinch here than at 60+ km in July.
- **Pace doesn't mask it.** The pinch is a fit/swell/cumulative-load mode, not a cardiovascular one — running slow won't hide a real problem. A clean Z1 run is genuine signal.

## How to read the result

- **Clean the whole way → positive but not conclusive.** 21 km is short of the 29 km where it first showed; on pre-loaded feet it's a decent proxy, but one clean sub-distance run doesn't fully clear the Megablast. Nudges the "Megablast fine" case forward.
- **Toe complains → clear Megablast signal.** The answer most worth having — converts the lingering mystery into "Megablast-specific fit problem," hardening the race-shoe lean toward the size-up Evo SL on the exact dimension the rotation already calls dominant (`schedule/2026-05-26-shoe-rotation.md`).

## Abort rule + what to record

If the pressure shows up, **note roughly the distance/time it started, then back off** — don't grind it out on a social Z1 run to "finish the test." *When* it starts is the key data point: earlier than May 29's 29 km = worse; later or never = better. Captured in tomorrow's sync.

## Bookkeeping

- This is a **single** Megablast exposure (Jun 5's long was Torin), so it does **not** trigger the back-to-back-Megablast calf rocker-load watch — that stays paused (`syncs/2026-06-05`).
- Forward plan structure, volume, and intensity are unchanged — this is a gear/test annotation on one session, not a plan rewrite.
