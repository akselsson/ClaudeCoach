# Schedule update — Kungsleden fastpack replaces the road 50+35

Supersedes the forward portion (Jul 13 onward) of `schedule/2026-07-06-weekly-review.md`. Not a retrospective review — the re-entry week (Jul 7–13) executed as drafted (Jul 8 34 km Evo SL long came back shin-clean; see `syncs/2026-07-08-activity-sync.md`). This locks in a decision made in conversation: swap the planned road first-big-B2B (50 km Evo SL + 35 km cushioned, Jul 18–19) for a two-day mountain fastpack of the southern Kungsleden.

## The decision

**The Kungsleden trip *is* the first big B2B — it replaces the 50+35, it does not stack on top of it.** ~77 km over two days with real fjäll vert is the season plan's first specificity B2B (weeks 7–10), moved five days earlier and swapped from road to trail.

Why it's a good trade, not a compromise:
- **Time-on-feet is the headline** — a 7 h day back-to-back with a 10 h day is premium 161 km specificity, more so than an 85 km road B2B split into two shorter efforts.
- **Balanced shape lowers single-day risk.** 36 / 41 (neither day crosses 50 km) is *safer* than the road 50+35 the plan penciled, and the terrain forces walk-breaks.
- **Day 2 on pre-fatigued legs is the feature** — it rehearses the back-half-of-the-race state a single fresh long run can't.
- **Direction flip (Hemavan→Ammarnäs) is deliberate:** the Syterskälet climb and exposed high pass are done fresh on Day 1 morning, then the route trends net-downhill toward Ammarnäs. Better than grinding the technical high point tired at km 50+.
- **Trail variation + vert** doubles as Lidingöloppet (Sep 26) leg-toughness and is gentler on the shins than repetitive road pounding.

Placement note: Jul 13/14 is Mon/Tue. The preferences' "leave Tuesday a clear day on each side" guardrail is **waived here** because Kundo is on summer break — there is no Tuesday group-quality anchor to protect for the rest of the build.

## The trip (Jul 13–15) — locked itinerary

**Shoe: Altra Lone Peak 9+ (`g31943887`), both days.** Low, wide, planted, broken-in trail primary — lowest ankle-roll risk on wet/rocky/off-camber fjäll, which is the decisive constraint. *Not* the Hoka Tecton X3: its dual carbon plate + tall stack is tippy on technical ground and the roll risk climbs as ankle proprioception fatigues on Day 2. The Tecton is a fast-runnable-trail tool, wrong for slow technical fjäll.

| Day | Legs | Dist | Moving | Night |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mon Jul 13** (start 13:00) | Hemavan → Viterskälet 10 → Syter 12 → Tärnasjö 14 | **36 km** | ~7 h | Tärnasjö hut |
| **Tue Jul 14** (start 08:00) | Tärnasjö → Serve 14 (lunch) → Aigert 19 → Ammarnäs 8 | **41 km** | ~10 h | Ammarnäs |
| **Wed Jul 15** | Bus 12:35 → Arvidsjaur 15:20 → fly ARN 18:10 | — | — | Home |

**Execution rules:**
- **Run Day 1's descents into Tärnasjö controlled; walk every climb.** Braking the downhills protects the quads for Day 2 *and* spares the lower legs — the one Lone Peak caveat (zero drop loads calf/Achilles more) is managed by descent control, and you're recently adapted to the shoe from Italy.
- **Both days Z1–Z2** (HR <150). Slow is the point.
- **Fuel like the race, from hour one — especially Day 2.** You start Day 2 glycogen-depleted, so it's the best fueling-under-fatigue rehearsal before Berlin. Carry Berlin race food; target 60–90 g carb/h. Treat it as data.
- **Bail-points are the huts.** Day 1: Viterskälet, Syter, Tärnasjö. Day 2: Serve, Aigert. Shins speak or legs cooked → stop at the next hut. Berlin > this trip, same rule always.
- **Pack light** — sleep/eat at the huts so you're fastpacking a vest, not carrying overnight kit on either big day.
- Daylight is a non-issue (far-north July), so the 20:00 Day-1 finish is fine — just confirm Tärnasjö takes a late check-in.
