# Shoe rotation by run intent

This file prescribes which shoe to wear for which run type, not what to run. Distances, HR zones, and day-of-week prescriptions in `2026-05-15-schedule-clarifications.md` (Block 1 Weeks 3–5) and `2026-05-03-season-plan-overview.md` (Block 2+ shape) are unchanged.

The trigger was the May 26 sync — three of the last four solo runs in the new Asics Megablast drifted from intended easy (<140) into steady (140–150) at the same HR but 20 s/km quicker pace than pre-shoe baselines. The Megablast is delivering free pace at equal effort; the cost is that Patrik isn't pocketing the saved effort as recovery. Rotation by run intent solves this without asking him to fight the shoe's bias.

## The rotation

| Run type | Shoe | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Easy / recovery / Thu mid-week | **Altra Torin** | Zero-drop + max cushion, no propulsion bias. The shoe should *help* the HR stay <140, not push pace. |
| Daughter long (weekend Z1, ~21 km) | **Altra Torin** | Confirmed working on May 24 — held 99.9% easy. External pace floor (daughter's cadence) also helps; the shoe doesn't fight that floor. |
| Tuesday quality (when group runs reps) | **Megablast** | Rocker + firm foam earn their keep at threshold/interval pace. The shoe's bias and the prescription agree at 5:00/km. |
| Friday Z2 long (24–32 km, Block 1) | **Megablast** | Z2 (HR 140–150) is exactly where the shoe naturally lands. Also tests rocker and forefoot foam under sustained load — early warning if calves/Achilles will complain at ultra distance. |
| B2B back half with daughter (Sat or Sun, ~21 km Z1) | **Altra Torin** | Same as the standalone daughter run. |
| **Block 2 specificity long runs (40 km+, starting Jun 22)** | **Adidas Evo SL** | The ultra audition. Bought one size up specifically for foot swell at hour 15+, which is the ultra-DNF risk Megablast can't address. Lightstrike Pro foam (PEBA-class) is responsive and protective; lower stack is more forgiving to a collapsing stride than Megablast's high stack. Needs distance to prove fit and foam — short runs don't tell you anything. |
| Mauerweglauf race day (Aug 15) | **Decided after the 60–70 km specificity runs in mid-Jul** | Both Megablast and Evo SL audition. The 50 / 60 / 70 km test runs are how the race shoe gets picked, not by reading specs. Survivor wins. |

## Why Evo SL is the leading ultra candidate (not yet the verdict)

Patrik bought the Evo SL one size up — that's not a sizing accident, it's a deliberate ultra choice. Foot swell at hour 15+ of a 161 km is significant; pinched toes, blisters, and black toenails at km 100+ are classic ultra DNF causes. The size-up *is* the feature. Megablast in normal fit doesn't address this.

Lightstrike Pro is also a real ultra-grade foam — PEBA-based, same family as the Adios Pro racer. Responsive enough at fresh legs, soft enough to protect when shot. Lower stack than Megablast means less leverage working against a tired stride.

Megablast's case for race day: more total foam = more impact protection on road for very long durations, and Blast Turbo foam may hold up better than PEBA over 160+ km. Not dismissable, but the size issue is the dominant factor unless the 60–70 km Evo SL runs surface a fit or foam problem.

## Fit-check run before promoting Evo SL to long-run duty

An upsized shoe can feel sloppy on short runs — foot slides without the swell to fill it, blistering from the *opposite* cause. Don't put Evo SL into short-easy duty.

**Fit check: Thu Jun 4 (Week 5's mid-week 14 km steady, HR 140–150).** Long enough for real load, short enough that a fit problem is recoverable. Watch for heel lift, midfoot slop, toe-box movement on downhills.

- **If clean** → promote Evo SL to Block 2 long-run-only duty starting Jun 22 onward.
- **If sloppy** → re-lace, try heel-lock lacing, retest on Sun Jun 7 easy. If still sloppy after that, the size-up was too aggressive and the ultra shoe decision narrows to Megablast (with Superblast 3 still in reserve as the backup).
