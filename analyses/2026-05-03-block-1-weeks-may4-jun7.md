# Block 1 — May 4 to Jun 7 (5 weeks)

Bridges post-Tuscany recovery into the Berlin Mauerweglauf base build. No quality work in weeks 1–3; first real session in week 5. Long run grows linearly. Cycling commutes count as aerobic recovery, not training load — keep them, but don't add a third commute day if the legs are tired.

## Heart-rate guideposts

Calibrate against your data — these are starting points, adjust after the first week.

- **Easy:** HR <140. Conversational. Most runs.
- **Steady aerobic:** HR 140–150. Long runs, medium-long midweek.
- **Threshold:** HR 155–165. Will reintroduce in week 5.
- **Race-effort cap this block:** HR 160. Anything above is a red flag — abort the session.

### Kungsholmen Runt race-day plan

- **Warm-up matters more than usual.** Fatigued legs need it. 15 min easy jog + 4× 30s strides + dynamic mobility. Start the race already warm.
- **Pacing: negative split or even.** Set your watch alarm if HR exceeds 165 in the first 7 km. Going out hot is the failure mode here.
  - Km 0–7: HR 150–160, controlled. Feels too easy on race day adrenaline. Trust it.
  - Km 7–17: HR 160–170, race effort.
  - Km 17–21: HR cap off. Empty the tank if it's there. **But:** if calves, Achilles, or shins start protesting, back off immediately. Don't trade Berlin for a Kungsholmen finish-line surge.
- **Fuel:** gel at km 8, gel at km 15. Half-marathons normally don't need this; today you do — your aerobic system is still working overtime on residual recovery.
- **Hard rule: no sprint finish.** Cross the line strong but don't kick. Last 200m sprints are how post-ultra runners pop a calf. You have nothing to prove against the clock today.
- **Bail criteria:** any sharp pain (not normal fatigue burn) = stop. A DNF here costs nothing. An injury costs Berlin.

### What "good" looks like Saturday

- Finishing feeling like you ran hard but could have run another 5 km if you had to.
- HR-to-pace ratio in the last 5 km not collapsing relative to the middle 10 km.
- Walking normally Sunday morning. Some soreness fine; limping is not.

### What "bad" looks like (and what we do)

- Sharp anterior shin / Achilles pain during the race → DNF, walk in. Week 2 becomes pure walking + spinning, push everything in Block 1 back another week.
- HR runaway by km 12 (HR climbing while pace drops) → switch to controlled finish, ignore the clock. Week 2 still goes per plan below.
- Feeling great, big PR vibes → don't chase it. Stick to the bail criteria. The race is a stimulus, not a test.

## What I'm watching for

Re-evaluate before locking Block 2 (week of Jun 8) on these signals:

- Resting HR trend through May. >5 bpm above your usual baseline persisting >3 days = back off.
- HR-to-pace drift at HR 140. Should be flat or improving by end of May. Worsening = under-recovered.
- Sleep quality. Ultra recovery shows up here first.
- Any niggle that lasts >3 days, especially Achilles or anterior shin (common after long mountain ultras).

If two of those go sideways, Block 2 starts with a deload week instead of progression.
