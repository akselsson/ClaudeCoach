## Stockholm Marathon — decision: skip

Entry not paid → no sunk cost, no reason to chase a second-hand bib. Skipping keeps the Berlin build clean: peak weekly volume can climb earlier and back-to-back weekends start in week 3 instead of week 5.

If FOMO hits the week before, the rule is: **buy a bib only if you commit to running it ≤ 5:00/km, HR <145, treating it as a Saturday long run with a finish line.** Anything else compromises Berlin.

## Berlin Mauerweglauf build (Aug 15)

From May 11 to Aug 15 = exactly 14 weeks of structured build. High-level shape:

- **Weeks 1–2 (May 11–24): residual recovery + reintroduction.** Volume capped at ~80% of pre-Tuscany peak. No quality. Reintroduce back-to-back long runs at the end of week 2.
- **Weeks 3–6 (May 25–Jun 21): aerobic base build.** Volume ramping to peak weekly, weekly long run climbing to 35–40 km, one mid-week 20–25 km medium-long. Weekly elevation matters for Mauerweglauf trail-ish profile (modest but cumulative). One quality session per week — threshold or hill strength, not VO2.
- **Weeks 7–10 (Jun 22–Jul 19): specificity.** Two big back-to-back weekends (e.g., 50 km + 35 km, then 60 km + 30 km). Night-running practice (Mauerweglauf has a night start or overnight section depending on the year). Race-pace work at goal 100-mile shuffle pace + walking-the-hills practice. Nutrition rehearsal at race food.
- **Weeks 11–12 (Jul 20–Aug 2): biggest weekend.** ~70 km + 40 km back-to-back, gear/nutrition fully rehearsed.
- **Weeks 13–14 (Aug 3–14): taper.** Sharp, not deep — ultras taper less than marathons. Volume −40% in week 13, −60% in week 14, intensity preserved.

## Post-Berlin → Lidingöloppet

- 5–6 weeks between Berlin and Lidingöloppet (Sep 26).
- Weeks 1–2 post-Berlin: full recovery, walks/easy spinning, jogging only when it feels right.
- Weeks 3–4: rebuild aerobic, reintroduce one short tempo.
- Week 5 (race week): light, race Lidingöloppet on whatever fitness remains. Don't try to PR — enjoy a fast 30k off ultra fitness.
