The three Lund mornings answered the question the block had been deferring: 21 km on day 1, 8 km easy with the wife on day 2, and 24.9 km solo on day-3 cumulative-fatigue feet. Toe-box perfect. Swelling a non-issue. Patrik's verdict was unambiguous. The fit-check that slipped three times is done — and the version that finally happened was a better test than any of the earlier plans.

New watch item: Patrik flagged the analogy to the Adidas Boston — a shoe that gave him shin-splint trouble once it started showing wear. Watch shins / medial-tibia specifically on longer Evo SL efforts from here, and track it against the shoe's accumulating mileage. If a medial-shin niggle surfaces, back the Evo SL off long efforts immediately and reopen the race-shoe question — there's still runway before Aug 15, but not if this surfaces in July uninvestigated.

Tuesday rule (updated Jun 14): the Kundo quality is taken as the group runs it — no HR-cap prescription. 4×4 ran VO₂ again this week (HR 146/172, Endorphin Speed) and was accepted. The old "threshold / >165 abort" rule is retired. Guards now: single weekly quality, everything else easy, long run protected, RHR watched. Endorphin Speed replaces the Megablast as the default Tuesday quality shoe; the Megablast is unpinned from Tuesdays (available ad-hoc).

Midfoot soreness (Jun 7 niggle): resolved without intervention across the full Lund volume, including the 24.9 km. Downgraded to resolved — re-flag only if it recurs under Italy trail/downhill load. The insole contingency is available but no longer a standing instruction.

Shoe rotation (Jun 21 update): easy / recovery / Thu → Altra Torin. Tuesday quality → Endorphin Speed. Post-Italy specificity runs 40 km+ → Adidas Evo SL Silver (sized up; the race shoe). Race-shoe verdict formalised after the 60–70 km specificity runs, target ~late Jul. Italy (Jun 22 – Jul 6) exception: Evo SL Silver stays home after a confirmed mild shin signal at 32 km on the Jun 21 long — two-week deload is the ideal rest window. Italy gear: Lone Peak 9+ primary (trail/hike — Como mountains, Cinque Terre coastal trails, Jun 24 family hike) + Megablast backup (road/recovery flat miles). Torin not packed (redundant zero-drop alongside Lone Peak). The gear-rotation proposal (Evo SL reserved for race day + race-specific longs; cushioned trainers for easy volume) is still a proposal — to be ratified at the Italy-return review.

Adopted May 10: 2× / week strength. Heavy stacks on Tuesday after the work-group session (squat / deadlift / lunge, 4–6 reps × 3–4 sets). Maintenance follows Thursday's easy / medium-long run (single-leg, hip / core / calf, 20–30 min). Run first, lift after — the run is the priority.

After Berlin · the second arc

Five to six weeks between a 161k and a fast 30k.
The body decides what's possible.

Weeks 1–2 post-Berlin are full recovery: walks, easy spinning, jogging only when it feels right. Weeks 3–4 rebuild aerobic and reintroduce one short tempo. Race week is light — Lidingö is run on whatever fitness remains.

The point is not to PR. It's to enjoy a fast 30k off ultra fitness while the body is still tuned. If recovery from Berlin runs longer than expected, Lidingöloppet drops to a hard long run with a finish line — Berlin was the year, and Lidingö is the cooldown.
