Saturday's half went hard — negative split, max HR 182, 45% in vo₂. The plan absorbs that cost across the next two weeks before quality returns. Below: the day-by-day for Block 1 (May 4 → Jun 7), then the strategic shape of the build to Berlin and the recovery into Lidingö.

Standing constraint: one weekend day each week is a committed easy ~21 km run with Patrik's daughter (Sat or Sun, her choice that week) — building toward Lidingöloppet at 6:00–6:30 /km. Lifts weekend volume by ~6–10 km over the original Block 1 estimates and gives most weeks a built-in B2B partner for the Friday long.

Adopted May 10: 2× / week strength. Heavy stacks on Tuesday after the work-group session (squat / deadlift / lunge variants, 4–6 reps × 3–4 sets). Maintenance follows Thursday's easy / medium-long run (single-leg, hip / core / calf, 20–30 min). Run first, lift after — the run is the priority, the strength serves it. Heavy drops in race weeks; maintenance only in post-race weeks.

After Berlin · the second arc

Five to six weeks between a 161k and a fast 30k.
The body decides what's possible.

Weeks 1–2 post-Berlin are full recovery: walks, easy spinning, jogging only when it feels right. Weeks 3–4 rebuild aerobic and reintroduce one short tempo. Race week is light — Lidingö is run on whatever fitness remains.

The point is not to PR. It's to enjoy a fast 30k off ultra fitness while the body is still tuned. If recovery from Berlin runs longer than expected, Lidingöloppet drops to a hard long run with a finish line — Berlin was the year, and Lidingö is the cooldown.
